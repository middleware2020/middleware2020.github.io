Venue Information

 

Aula Conference Center, TU Delft 

Middleware 2020 will be held in the Aula Conference Center, located in the heart of the TU Delft campus. It offers a variety of different meeting rooms, an auditorium, various facilities, and large exhibition areas. A bar and a buffet restaurant are located on the ground floor. All rooms of the TU Delft Conference Centre are equipped with necessary technology (screen, computer, connectors). Eduroam and conference wifi is available. The building is fully accessible.

Bicyling

The bicycle is the traditional mode of transportation in the Netherlands. Delft has an extensive network of bike lanes, including some dedicated roads for bicycles. Most public places have a large number of bike racks for parking. Be mindful that bike lanes and cycle paths can be very busy during the peak hours and you should be reasonably experienced when cycling in dense traffic. Information about the most important traffic rules for bicycles can be found on www.holland.com.

Bikes can be rented from various places in the city. The orange bikes from Mobike can be unlocked and used through their mobile app

Public Transport

The Aula Conference Center can easily be reached with public transport. The bus stop TU Aula is in close proximity to the building. At this moment, it is served by the bus lines 40 and 69 which both depart from the central station. Due to construction work on a new bridge, the buses currently take a detour and take about 20 minutes to reach the TU Campus. From mid 2020, they should run the normal route again and the travel time is reduced to 5 minutes. From end of 2020, the bus line 69 will be replaced by the extended Tram line 19. Line 174 also served the stop and connects with the Metro Line E in Pijnacker Zuid. 

Plan your Trip

In order to plan your trip, you can use the travel planner 9292.nl or the app of the national railway company NS which also contains local public transport information www.ns.nl

Pay your Fare

While it is possible to use one-way disposable tickets at vending stations and, in some forms of transportation, from the driver, you will be charged a surcharge of 1 € per ticket. Consider purchasing an OV Chipkaart. 

 

Campus 

TU Delft's campus is one of the largest in the world and spans 161 hectars. It features the faculty buildings as well as common spaces like the sport facilities Delft X and the mixed educational building Pulse. The west side of the campus whith the Aula building is directly adjacent to the old town of Delft. A map of the entire campus can be found here.

About 27.000 people travel across our campus every day: students, scientist, visitors, and employees of the university and the companies on campus. Stretching over 161 hectares, the campus is larger than downtown Delft, and one of the largest university campuses in the world. The campus has been equipped with an extensive bicycle and pedestrian road network. The TU Delft campus has a green, parky character. Large parts of the campus are therefore only accessible on foot, by bike or public transport. For motorists there is the campus ring road, which circles the entire campus. Clear signage on the campus ring guides you easily to the central parking lots. From there, every destination is reachable on foot.
